## Why distributed systems feel different

Once machines communicate over the network, partial failure becomes normal instead of exceptional. Messages can arrive late, out of order, or not at all.

## Concepts to internalize

### Replication

Replication improves durability and availability, but you need to decide who is allowed to accept writes and how conflicts are resolved.

### Consistency

Strong consistency simplifies reasoning for some products, while eventual consistency improves availability and latency in others. The right answer depends on the user promise.

### Partition tolerance

Real networks partition. Production systems must define how they behave when communication between nodes becomes unreliable.

<InterviewTip title="Tie theory back to product behavior">
Instead of reciting CAP theorem terminology, describe what users experience during failures. Can they still read? Can they write? Will they see stale data? That framing lands better in interviews.
</InterviewTip>

## Practical advice

- Prefer simpler coordination models first.
- Isolate failure domains.
- Make retries idempotent.
- Invest in observability before scale forces you to.
